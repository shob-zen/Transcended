LOG 02: FRACTURE

VOLUME 1: TRANSCENDED

The name The Primes echoed in Zen’s mind like a death knell. He looked at his hands, which were still glowing with a faint, pulsing light—the remnants of the radiation that had claimed his world.

"Who are they?" Zen asked, his voice a low growl. "And why would they destroy a planet that did nothing to them?"

Bruce stood up, his massive frame casting a long shadow over the lunar dust. "The Primes believe in universal evolution through destruction. They believe that only the strongest species should occupy the stars. They didn't see Earth as a home; they saw it as a failed experiment."

Zen felt a fracture in his soul. The grief was still there, but it was being rapidly overtaken by a cold, hard necessity. He couldn't just sit on the moon and wait to fade away. He needed to be stronger. He needed to be like Bruce.

"Teach me," Zen said, standing up. He struggled to keep his feet on the ground as the low gravity of the moon tried to pull his "light" body upward. "Teach me how to control this. How to fight them."

Bruce nodded slowly. "It won't be easy, kid. You’re a Sentinel now—or at least, you have the potential to be. But your power is raw. You’re like a star that hasn't learned how to burn without exploding."

They boarded the ship, a vessel of shimmering alloy that Zen later learned was called the Aegis-7. Within hours, they arrived at the Sentinel Training Camp, a massive station hidden within the asteroid belt of a distant system. There, Zen was introduced to Daniel, a senior tactical member known as the Star Vessel.

Daniel looked Zen up and down with a cocky smirk. "So this is the survivor? He looks like he’d break if I hit him with a drill fist."

"Don't underestimate him, Daniel," Bruce warned. "He’s survived a direct Gamma Burst. He has the weight of a world in his hands."

Zen didn't say a word. He just stared at the training drones buzzing in the hangar. The training was about to begin, and the path to becoming The Transcended had finally opened.
