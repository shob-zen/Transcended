Chapter 2: Fracture

Three months later...

Zen was now stationed at the Solar Sentinels' training camp on the planet Omnis-7. His body had changed significantly; surviving the gamma-ray burst without a scratch had granted him unimaginable strength. Inside the training chamber, he was a blur of motion, dodging high-velocity targets and dismantling combat drones to sharpen his reflexes.

A heavy sliding door hissed open, and Bruce walked in. "In only three months, you’ve put on some serious muscle, kid."

Zen wiped sweat from his brow. "So, am I ready?"

"My team is currently tracking the latest string of attacks by The Primes," Bruce replied, motioning for Zen to follow. "Come with me. I’ll show you."

They left the chamber and walked through a sprawling hallway. Through massive reinforced glass, Zen could see the futuristic skyline of Omnis-7, while maintenance robots scurried about, repairing damage from recent skirmishes. They reached a secure gate equipped with a biometric scanner. Bruce placed his hand on the sensor, and the doors slid open to reveal the Sentinels' Control Room.

"What is all this?" Zen asked, staring at the rows of holographic screens.

"This is the nerve center of the Solar Sentinels," a robotic voice replied. "Every threat in the known universe is detected and analyzed here."

Bruce stepped up to a console, swiping through data until he pulled up a projection of the planet Apricon. "There’s an active attack on the civilization there. Get ready, Zen. You’re going in."

Zen hesitated. "How am I supposed to fight a Prime with just three months of training?"

"Don’t worry," Bruce said, looking at a data pad. "My team has been analyzing your stats. We believe you’ve already reached semi-Prime levels of power."

Scene Shift: Solar Sentinel Spacecraft – Orbiting Apricon

"You won't be going in alone," Bruce said over the ship's comms as they entered the planet's atmosphere.

"Then who’s my backup?" Zen asked.

A sliding door at the back of the cabin popped open, and a figure in sleek armor stepped out. The helmet’s metal plates retracted and dissolved into the suit, revealing a young man’s face.

"This is Daniel," Bruce introduced. "He’s a newly promoted senior team member. He’ll be your guide against the Prime."

"Hey. You must be Zen. I’ve heard a lot about you," Daniel said with a grin.

"I guess so," Zen replied, eyeing Daniel's gear.

"I’m known as Star Vessel," Daniel explained. "This is a nano-technology suit. It operates on direct neural commands from my brain." He tapped a sleek wristband, and the suit retracted entirely into the band. "See? I can suit up in a heartbeat."

Daniel reached into his pocket and pulled out a space-colored ring engraved with a star symbol. "Take this. A welcome gift for joining the team."

Zen slipped the ring onto his finger. Before he could thank him, Bruce’s voice crackled over the speakers. "Eyes up, both of you. We’ve reached Apricon. Get ready to deploy."

Daniel swiped his wristband, and the nano-tech flowed over his body like liquid metal. "Hey, moron," Daniel joked, "aren't you going to press the ring?"

Zen pressed the crest of the ring. Instantly, a high-tech combat suit materialized around him. With their helmets locked, the two heroes launched out of the ship’s bay and into the void.

"Apricon is surrounded by a dense asteroid belt," Bruce explained in their ears. "I’m dropping you outside the ring so I don't wreck the ship."

"Just admit you don't know how to park this old bucket, Bruce," Daniel teased.

Zen laughed as Bruce grumbled a "whatever" over the line. They dove into the asteroid field, weaving through jagged rocks at terminal velocity. Zen’s training paid off; he dodged the debris with effortless grace.

"Woah, dude! You’re fast," Daniel shouted, engaging his suit's boosters to keep up.

They breached the stratosphere and descended toward the surface. "Hey Bruce, my tracker is coming up empty," Daniel reported. "I don't see any cities."

"That’s because Apricon is a wasteland," Bruce replied. "The civilization lives underground to escape the sandstorms."

They touched down on the desert floor. Daniel pointed toward a massive fissure in the earth. "I’ve got a reading on high-density structures down there. Let’s move."

They entered a massive subterranean tunnel. As they reached the end, Zen gasped. Before them lay a sprawling metropolis where buildings hung from the cavern ceiling and rose from the floor.

"This is crazy," Daniel muttered. "How did they even build these upside down?"

"Have you ever actually fought a Prime before?" Zen asked quietly.

Daniel’s tone turned serious. "Nope. But I’ve heard the stories. They’re way stronger than our standard forces. Our three top seniors died taking down just two of them." Daniel paused, picking up a rock to scan for energy signatures. Suddenly, he froze. He grabbed Zen by the shoulder and yanked him behind a massive pillar. "Did you feel that?"

"Yeah," Zen whispered, his heart racing. "A massive pressure."

Zen peered around the edge of the rock and went pale. The city was silent, save for the wind. The streets were littered with the bodies of small, alien civilians. Suddenly, a violent gust of wind swept through the plaza, tossing the bodies aside like autumn leaves.

Someone was standing there.

The figure stood atop the skull of a fallen alien. With a mere flex of power, the body beneath his feet vanished into a spray of blood, leaving nothing but the bone. As the dust settled, the terrifying silhouette of a PRIME was revealed.
