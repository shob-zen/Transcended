LOG 01: GROUNDED

VOLUME 1: TRANSCENDED

The story begins in 2024. In New York City, robberies were increasing daily until a new street hero suddenly emerged. Clad in a combat suit with two "mini horns," he intercepted a group of thieves attempting to steal a briefcase from a casino. Soon, the public knew him by one name: Slasher.

Slasher became a massive influence on the younger generation, including a seventeen-year-old boy named Zen. With his eighteenth birthday only two months away, Zen lived a very ordinary life and felt a desperate urge to change it.

That change came, but not in the way he expected.

His parents called him into the living room to see a breaking news report: a massive gamma radiation burst was headed for Earth. The impact was cataclysmic. The burst tore through the planet, destroying half of the Earth and a large portion of the moon.

Zen managed to hug his parents one last time before their bodies turned to ash in his arms. Devastated and confused, Zen realized he had somehow survived the radiation. As he stood amongst the ruins, he noticed his feet felt strangely light, while his grip and fists felt incredibly heavy. The moment he relaxed his legs, he began to float.

Caught in the momentum, Zen soared straight into the sky, eventually reaching the vacuum of outer space. Overwhelmed by loneliness, he landed on the remains of the moon. As he stood on the lunar surface, he watched a massive spaceship descend. A bearded man stepped out.

"Let’s talk for a while," the man said. He picked up a massive boulder as if it weighed nothing, placed it down, and took a seat. "My name is Bruce. I am sorry to hear about the destruction of your planet."

Bruce leaned forward. "I am from the Solar Sentinels. We are the guardians who protect the galaxies."

"Then why didn't you come to protect mine?!" Zen demanded, his voice trembling with grief.

"I am sorry, kid," Bruce replied solemnly. "But this was not a natural disaster."

Zen’s grief turned to rage. "Then who did it?"

Bruce looked him in the eye. "The Primes."
